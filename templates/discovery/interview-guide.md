# Interview Guide

> This guide structures discovery interviews around Jobs-to-be-Done principles.
> The goal is to understand what people are trying to accomplish, what is getting in the way, and what "better" looks like to them -- not to validate our product idea.
>
> **Golden rule:** Listen more than you talk. Follow the energy. Ask "why" and "tell me more" relentlessly.

## Core Questions

_Ask these in order, but follow the participant's energy. If they go deep on a topic, stay with it._

### Q1: Context & Role
**JTBD Dimension:** Context & Circumstances
**Hypothesis link:** Background / all hypotheses

> "Can you tell me about your role and what a typical week looks like for you?"

_Probes: What takes up most of your time? What is the most important part of your job? What does success look like in your role?_

---

### Q2: Current Workflow
**JTBD Dimension:** Functional Job
**Hypothesis link:** H1

> "Walk me through how you currently handle [the process/task we are investigating]. Start from the beginning -- what triggers it, and what happens step by step?"

_Probes: Who else is involved? What tools do you use? How long does it typically take? What does the output look like?_

---

### Q3: Pain Points
**JTBD Dimension:** Push of the Situation
**Hypothesis link:** H1, H2

> "What is the most frustrating part of this process? What makes you think 'there has to be a better way'?"

_Probes: How often does this happen? What is the impact when it goes wrong? Can you give me a specific recent example?_

---

### Q4: Workarounds
**JTBD Dimension:** Functional Job / Current Habits
**Hypothesis link:** H2

> "Have you tried to solve this problem yourself? What workarounds or hacks have you put in place?"

_Probes: How did you come up with that approach? How well does it work? What are the downsides?_

---

### Q5: Desired Outcome
**JTBD Dimension:** Desired Progress
**Hypothesis link:** H3

> "If you could wave a magic wand and this process worked perfectly, what would that look like? What would be different?"

_Probes: How would you measure "better"? What would you do with the time you save? How would your team benefit?_

---

### Q6: Switching Triggers
**JTBD Dimension:** Push + Pull Forces
**Hypothesis link:** H4

> "Have you ever looked for a tool or solution to help with this? What prompted you to look? What happened?"

_Probes: What did you search for? What did you find? Why did you choose it / not choose it? What was the deciding factor?_

---

### Q7: Barriers to Change
**JTBD Dimension:** Anxiety + Habit Forces
**Hypothesis link:** H4, H5

> "What would make it hard for you to adopt a new approach or tool for this, even if it was clearly better?"

_Probes: Budget? Integration? Learning curve? Team buy-in? Risk? What has stopped you before?_

---

### Q8: Value & Prioritization
**JTBD Dimension:** Emotional Job / Stakes
**Hypothesis link:** H3, H5

> "How important is solving this problem compared to other things on your plate? Where does it rank?"

_Probes: Would you invest time to fix this? Budget? What would need to be true for this to become a top priority? Who else cares about this problem?_

## Follow-up Questions

_Use these to go deeper on signals that emerge during core questions._

1. **"Tell me more about that."** -- The most powerful follow-up. Use it generously.

2. **"Can you give me a specific example?"** -- Moves from generalization to concrete reality.

3. **"What happened next?"** -- Keeps the story going when they pause.

4. **"You mentioned [X]. Why is that important to you?"** -- Reveals underlying motivation.

5. **"How did that make you feel?"** -- Gets at emotional dimension of the job.

6. **"Who else is affected by this? How do they feel about it?"** -- Reveals social dimension and stakeholder dynamics.

---

## Disconfirming Questions

> These questions are designed to challenge our assumptions. Ask them even when (especially when) the interview seems to confirm your hypothesis.

1. **"What if this problem just went away on its own -- is that possible? What would cause that?"**
   _Purpose: Tests whether the problem is truly persistent or situational._

2. **"What is actually working well about your current approach? What would you miss if it changed?"**
   _Purpose: Reveals attachment to current solution and switching cost we might underestimate._

3. **"If someone offered you a perfect solution for free, what reasons might you still have for not using it?"**
   _Purpose: Uncovers barriers beyond price -- integration, trust, habit, organizational inertia._

## Wrap-up Protocol (5 minutes)

> "We are coming to the end of our time. A few final questions:"

1. "Is there anything I should have asked but did not?"
2. "Is there anyone else on your team or in your network who would be good for us to speak with?"
3. "Would it be okay if we followed up with a short survey or a brief follow-up call in a few weeks?"

> "Thank you so much for your time. Your input is incredibly valuable and will directly shape what we build. [Explain incentive delivery if applicable.]"

## Initial Codebook

_Use these tags consistently when coding interview notes. Add new tags as they emerge._

| Tag | Definition | Look For |
|-----|------------|----------|
| `pain-point` | Explicit frustration or problem | Emotional language, sighing, "the worst part is..." |
| `workaround` | Manual or improvised solution | "What I do is...", "I built a spreadsheet for...", "My hack is..." |
| `desired-outcome` | What they wish they could achieve | "I wish I could...", "In a perfect world...", "What I really need is..." |
| `switching-trigger` | What caused or would cause a change | "The last straw was...", "When [X] happened, I started looking for..." |
| `barrier` | What prevents adopting a new solution | "The problem with switching is...", "We tried but...", "My boss would never..." |
| `jtbd-functional` | The practical task they are trying to accomplish | Action verbs, process descriptions, step-by-step narratives |
| `jtbd-emotional` | How they want to feel | "I want to feel confident that...", "It stresses me out when..." |
| `jtbd-social` | How they want to be perceived | "My team thinks I...", "My boss expects...", "I need to look like I..." |
| `surprise` | Something unexpected | Anything that contradicts your hypotheses or assumptions |

## Interviewer Reminders

- **Do not pitch.** You are here to learn, not sell.
- **Do not lead.** Ask open questions. Never suggest the answer you want.
- **Silence is good.** After they answer, wait 3-5 seconds. They will often elaborate.
- **Follow the energy.** If they light up or get frustrated about something, dig in.
- **Seek disconfirmation.** The most valuable insight is the one that proves you wrong.
- **Specific > general.** "Tell me about the last time..." beats "Do you usually...?"
- **Behavior > opinion.** What they do matters more than what they say they would do.
