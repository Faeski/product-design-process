# Hypothesis Sheet

> Hypotheses are falsifiable statements about our beliefs. They exist to be tested, not confirmed.
> A good hypothesis makes it clear what would prove us wrong. If nothing could prove it wrong, it is not a hypothesis -- it is an assumption hiding as a fact.

---

## How to Write a Good Hypothesis

**Format:**
> We believe **[user group]** struggles with **[problem]** in the context of **[circumstance]**.
> **True if** [observable evidence that would confirm this].
> **False if** [observable evidence that would disprove this].
> **Cheapest test:** [the fastest, lowest-cost way to gather evidence].
> **Stop criteria:** [what would make us abandon this direction entirely].

**Rules:**
- Every hypothesis must be falsifiable -- define what "false" looks like before testing
- Prioritize hypotheses that, if wrong, would change your direction
- Update status honestly: do not stretch evidence to fit your belief
- One strong disconfirming signal outweighs multiple weak confirming signals

> **Multi-persona note:** Hypotheses can target a single persona or span across personas. Cross-persona hypotheses are valid and often critical -- for example, "the handoff between Persona A and Persona B causes delays and information loss." Use the Persona Linkage field to indicate which persona(s) each hypothesis relates to.
